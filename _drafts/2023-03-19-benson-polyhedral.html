In this post, we will see all the details of the proof that the class of Benson polyhedral and semilinear sets are equivalent. Moreover, we will see that semilinear sets have rational multivariate generating functions.

Definitions

A set subset of \(\mathbb{Z}^k\) is called semilinear if it can be written as a finite union of linear sets of the form \[ L(\vec u,\{\vec v_1,\vec v_2,...,\vec v_p\}) = \{ \vec u + k_1 \vec v_1 + k_2 \vec v_2 + \cdots + k_p \vec v_p \mid k_1,k_2,...,k_p\in \mathbb{N} \} \] where \(\vec u,\vec v_1,...,\vec v_p \in \mathbb{Z}^k\). {% include cite.html cites="is1" %}
