In this post, we will see all the details of the proof that the class of Benson polyhedral and semilinear sets are equivalent. Moreover, we will see that semilinear sets have rational multivariate generating functions.

Definitions

Definition 1 (Semilinear sets): A subset of \(\mathbb{Z}^k\) is called semilinear if it can be written as a finite union of linear sets of the form \[ L(\vec u,\{\vec v_1,\vec v_2,...,\vec v_p\}) = \{ \vec u + k_1 \vec v_1 + k_2 \vec v_2 + \cdots + k_p \vec v_p \mid k_1,k_2,...,k_p\in \mathbb{N} \} \] where \(\vec u,\vec v_1,...,\vec v_p \in \mathbb{Z}^k\).

Definition (Unambiguous semilinear): We say that a semilinear set is unambiguous if it can be written as a disjoint union \[ \bigcup_{i=1}^m L(\vec u_i,\{\vec v_{i,1},\vec v_{1,2},...,\vec v_{i,p_i}\}) \] where each set \(\{\vec v_{i,1},\vec v_{1,2},...,\vec v_{i,p_i}\}\) is linearly independent as vectors in \(\mathbb{Q}^k\).

Definition 2 (Polyhedral sets): A subset of \(\mathbb{Z}^k\) is called (Benson) polyhedral if it can be written using unions and intersections of sets of the form \[ \{ \vec z\in \mathbb{Z}^k \mid \vec a \cdot \vec z \geqslant b \} \] and \[ \{ \vec z \in \mathbb{Z}^k \mid \vec a \cdot \vec z \equiv b\ \mathrm{mod}\ c \} \] where \(\vec a \in \mathbb{Z}^k\) and \(b,c \in \mathbb{Z}\).
